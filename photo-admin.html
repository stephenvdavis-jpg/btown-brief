Before you tap approve — the five checks

  • Kids: identifiable children need a parent behind the submission. A stranger's photo of someone else's kid: reject.
  • Private property: shot from the street is fine; into a window, over a fence, or in someone's yard is not.
  • Bad days: no crashes, fires, arrests, medical emergencies, or people visibly in crisis. That's news; this is a gallery.
  • Would they expect it? People as scenery is fine. A close-up of a stranger who didn't know: reject, or crop-worthy at best.
  • AI or heavy edits: not a reason to reject — but label it so readers know.

When in doubt, reject. Removal requests (email) always win: find the photo under Recent and hit Remove.
